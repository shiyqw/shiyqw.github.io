Checkpoint summary

The general goal of our project is to implement a lock-free concurrent hashmap in C++. In the TA's feedback, we are encouraged to complemete more work rather than simply implementing a single hashmap data structure. Now we modified our goals a bit. In this project, we will implement lock-free concurrent hashmap in C++ with different collision resolution methods and evaluate their performances. In specific, we are trying to implement our lock-free concurrent hashmap with linear probing, leapfrog probing and cuckoo hashing. If still have free time, we will try to extend our implementations on more collision resolution methods.

Complete Work

As scheduled, the first week we focused on background study and deciding the base implementation method. We've read several related work. The following is the list of the materials we have read.
(1) A Lock-Free Wait-Free Hash Table
(2) Lock-Free Data Structures with Hazard Pointers
(3) The World's Simplest Lock-Free Hash Table
(4) A Non-Blocking HashTable
After discussion we decided to base our implementation on the method in the forth material. This method have a Java implementation called NonBlockingHashMap. We are trying to re-implemented it on top of C++. We are still working on it until now.
We recieved our feedback on the first days of the second week. We discussed several available extensions of our works and we decided to focus our extension on different collision resolution methods. We researched on several different collision resolution methods and decided to choose linear probing, leapfrog probing and cuckoo hashing as our goal functions.
